Understanding Your Water Meter

As a homeowner in Morgan Hill, CA, an unexpectedly high water bill can be alarming. One of the best ways to check for potential leaks is by reading your water meter. Knowing how to read your meter can save you money and prevent damage to your home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to accurately read your water meter, identify leaks, and understand when it's time to call in the professionals at Bunnies Plumbing & Trenchless Technology.

Types of Water Meters

Before diving into how to read your water meter, it's important to know that there are two main types of meters:

  • Analog Meters: These mechanical meters have a series of dials that indicate your water usage. Each dial represents a different digit.
  • Digital Meters: These electronic meters display your water usage digitally, making it easier to read and understand.

Steps to Read Your Water Meter

Reading your water meter is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to get an accurate reading:

  1. Locate your water meter, usually found near the front of your property or in a basement.
  2. For analog meters, read the dials from left to right. Note the number where the needle points, and remember that if the needle is between numbers, you record the lower number.
  3. If you have a digital meter, simply note the number displayed on the screen.
  4. Record this reading, and compare it to your last reading to calculate your water usage over a specific period.

Checking for Leaks

Once you have your readings, it's time to check for leaks. Here’s how:

  • Take an initial reading before going to bed and another reading first thing in the morning. Make sure no water is being used in your home during this time.
  • If the readings differ, you likely have a leak somewhere in your plumbing system.
  • Common signs of leaks include:
    • Unexplained puddles in your yard or around the meter
    • Increased water bills without increased usage
    • Running water sounds when no fixtures are in use

When to Call a Professional

If you suspect a leak and have followed the above steps, it may be time to contact a licensed plumber.
