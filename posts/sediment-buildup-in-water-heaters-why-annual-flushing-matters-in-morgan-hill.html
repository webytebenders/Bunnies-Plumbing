Understanding Sediment Buildup

Water heaters work by heating water to a desired temperature, but as water is heated, minerals like calcium and magnesium can settle at the bottom of the tank. Over time, this buildup can:

  • Reduce heating efficiency, causing your unit to work harder and consume more energy.
  • Lead to corrosion and rusting of the tank, which can result in leaks.
  • Cause unpleasant odors or tastes in your hot water supply.

In the Bay Area, where water hardness can vary, sediment issues are common, making regular flushing crucial for maintaining your water heater's performance.

Signs Your Water Heater Needs Flushing

So how do you know if your water heater is due for a flush? Watch for these signs:

  1. Unusual noises: If your water heater starts to make rumbling or popping sounds, this could be a sign of sediment buildup.
  2. Discolored or cloudy water: If your hot water appears rusty or cloudy, sediment may be affecting its quality.
  3. Increased energy bills: A sudden spike in your energy costs can indicate your heater is working overtime due to sediment.
  4. Temperature fluctuations: If your hot water isnâ€™t consistently warm, sediment could be hindering the heating process.

If you notice any of these signs, contact a licensed plumber right away to assess the situation.
