Understanding Sewer Camera Inspections

A sewer camera inspection is a vital tool for diagnosing plumbing problems that are otherwise invisible to the naked eye. If you’ve noticed signs like slow drains, foul odors, or soggy patches in your yard, it might be time to consider bringing in a professional to conduct a sewer camera inspection. This advanced technology can save you time and money by pinpointing the exact location and nature of the plumbing issues.

Why Consider a Sewer Camera Inspection?

There are several reasons why homeowners in Morgan Hill, CA, might need a sewer camera inspection:

  • Frequent Clogs: If you’re dealing with repeated clogs despite using store-bought drain cleaners, a camera inspection can identify the root cause.
  • Foul Odors: Unpleasant smells in or around your home may indicate a broken sewer line. A camera can help locate the source.
  • Yard Issues: Soggy patches or unusually lush grass can signify a leak in your sewer line.
  • Home Buying: If you’re purchasing a new home, a sewer inspection can prevent future headaches by revealing hidden problems.

How a Sewer Camera Inspection Works

During a sewer camera inspection, our licensed technicians at Bunnies Plumbing & Trenchless Technology insert a small, waterproof camera into your sewer line. This camera transmits real-time video back to our monitors, allowing us to see the condition of the pipes without invasive digging.

This method is particularly beneficial for identifying:

  • Cracks and breaks in pipes
  • Tree root intrusions
  • Corrosion or buildup
  • Improperly installed pipes

DIY vs. Professional Inspections

While some homeowners might consider DIY methods for inspecting their plumbing, such as using a basic drain snake, these methods lack the precision and insight of a professional sewer camera inspection. Attempting to diagnose plumbing issues without proper tools can lead to further damage and higher repair costs down the line.

For example, if you suspect tree roots are invading your sewer lines, a simple snake won’t reveal the full extent of the damage. In contrast, a camera can provide a clear view of how deep the roots are and how much they’ve compromised the pipes.

Signs You Need a Sewer Camera Inspection

Here are some key indicators that it’s time to schedule a sewer camera inspection:

  1. Repeated clogs in multiple drains.
  2. Foul odors coming from your drains.
  3. Slow drainage in sinks, tubs, or toilets.
  4. Soggy patches in your yard, particularly near the sewer line.
  5. Unexplained increases in your water bill.

If you notice any of these signs, contact a licensed plumber right away to prevent further damage.
