What Causes Sewage Backups?

Sewage backups can occur for various reasons, including:

  • Clogged Drains: Hair, grease, and foreign objects can create blockages.
  • Tree Roots: Roots can infiltrate sewer lines, causing blockages and damage.
  • Old or Damaged Pipes: Aging pipes may crack or collapse, leading to backups.
  • Heavy Rain: Excessive rainfall can overwhelm sewer systems, especially in low-lying areas.

Understanding the cause of a sewage backup is essential for determining the appropriate solution. While some homeowners may attempt to clear minor clogs with a plunger or drain cleaner, significant problems often require professional intervention.

Immediate Steps to Take During a Sewage Backup

If you find yourself facing a sewage backup, take the following steps immediately:

  1. Stop using all water fixtures to prevent further backup.
  2. Turn off your home's main water supply to minimize damage.
  3. Use towels or mops to absorb any standing water, if safe to do so.
  4. Contact a licensed plumber, like Bunnies Plumbing & Trenchless Technology, for emergency assistance.
