Understanding Water Heater Leaks

If you've recently noticed water pooling around your water heater, you're likely feeling a mix of concern and urgency. A leaking water heater can lead to significant water damage in your home, mold growth, and costly repairs. The good news is that understanding the common causes of these leaks can help you address the issue before it escalates. Common Causes of Water Heater Leaks

Water heaters can leak for a variety of reasons, and identifying the source is crucial for effective repair. Here are some of the most common causes:

  • Corrosion: Over time, the interior of the tank can corrode, especially if you have hard water. This rust can create holes, leading to leaks.
  • Loose Connections: The plumbing connections to the water heater can become loose due to temperature fluctuations, causing leaks at the joints.
  • Faulty Pressure Relief Valve: This valve releases excess pressure. If it's malfunctioning, it can leak water.
  • Temperature and Pressure (T&P) Relief Valve Issues: If this valve is malfunctioning, it may leak water or allow steam to escape.
  • Drain Valve Issues: If the drain valve is cracked or not sealed correctly, it can leak water.
