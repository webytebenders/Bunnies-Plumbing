Understanding the Unexpected Spike in Your Water Bill

As a homeowner in Morgan Hill, CA, you might experience moments of shock when you open your water bill and find it significantly higher than usual. This sudden increase can stem from various hidden issues within your plumbing system. Identifying these problems early can save you not only money but also prevent potential damage to your property. In this article, we'll explore common causes of high water bills, providing you with practical tips and professional solutions.

Common Causes of a High Water Bill

  • Leaky Faucets and Toilets: A dripping faucet can waste over 3,000 gallons of water per year. Similarly, a running toilet can use up to 200 gallons a day. Regularly check for these leaks, and consider our guide on how to fix a dripping faucet.
  • Water Main Issues: Problems with your water main can lead to increased water usage. Look for signs like damp spots in your yard or unexplained puddles. Our team specializes in water main line services to diagnose and repair these issues.
  • Hidden Leaks: Leaks behind walls or under floors can go unnoticed for long periods, drastically increasing your water bill. If you suspect a hidden leak, itâ€™s crucial to seek professional help.
  • Increased Usage: Seasonal changes can lead to increased water use. For instance, watering your garden more frequently during the summer might cause your bill to rise.
  • Malfunctioning Appliances: Appliances like dishwashers and washing machines can consume more water when they malfunction. Regular maintenance is key.

Identifying Water Leaks in Your Home

To pinpoint leaks, start by observing your water meter. Here's a simple method:

  1. Turn off all water sources in your home, including irrigation systems.
  2. Record the reading on your water meter.
  3. Wait for two hours without using any water, then check the meter again.
  4. If the reading has changed, you likely have a leak.

If you discover a leak, contact a licensed plumber right away to prevent extensive damage.

When to Consider Professional Plumbing Services

While some plumbing issues can be DIY projects, others require the expertise of a professional. Here are scenarios when you should definitely call a plumber:

  • You notice signs of water damage, such as mold or mildew.
  • Your yard is consistently soggy, indicating a possible sewer line issue.
  • You detect a sudden drop in water pressure, which might hint at a major leak.
  • There's a persistent musty smell indicating a hidden leak, possibly in your crawl space. Check out our post on crawl space moisture problems.
