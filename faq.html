Trenchless Technology

Trenchless sewer repair is a modern method of replacing or repairing underground sewer pipes without digging large trenches. Using pipe bursting or pipe lining (CIPP) technology, we access the damaged pipe through small entry points, preserving your yard, driveway, and landscaping. Learn more on our Trenchless Technology page.

Yes — in many cases, trenchless is actually more effective. The HDPE pipes used in pipe bursting have a lifespan of 50+ years and are resistant to tree root intrusion, corrosion, and cracking. Pipe lining creates a seamless, jointless pipe-within-a-pipe that eliminates leak points. Both methods meet or exceed all local building codes.

Most trenchless sewer replacements are completed in 1–3 days, compared to 1–2 weeks for traditional excavation. Simple pipe lining jobs can sometimes be completed in a single day. The exact timeline depends on the length of pipe, accessibility, and specific conditions at your property.

Trenchless repair is often comparable or even less expensive than traditional methods when you factor in the total cost. Traditional excavation requires additional expenses for landscape restoration, driveway repair, tree replacement, and longer labor hours. With trenchless, you avoid all of these restoration costs.

No! That's the biggest advantage of trenchless technology. We only need to dig 1–2 small access points (typically 3–4 feet each) at the entry and exit of the pipe section. Your lawn, trees, driveway, and landscaping remain completely intact. We backfill and clean up the access points when finished.
